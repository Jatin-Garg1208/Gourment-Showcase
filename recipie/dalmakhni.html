Dal Makhani

Dal Makhani is a beloved Indian dish that celebrates the richness of lentils and spices. To start, soak a cup of whole black lentils (urad dal) and a quarter cup of red kidney beans (rajma) for at least 4-6 hours or overnight.
After draining them, pressure cook the lentils and beans until they're soft and well-cooked, then mash some of the lentils to thicken the mixture. In a separate pan, saut√© finely chopped onions in ghee or butter until they turn golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste, chopped green chili, and a puree of two tomatoes along with spices like red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook this aromatic mixture until the oil begins to separate.
Combine this masala with the cooked lentils and beans, letting it simmer on low heat. After about 20-30 minutes, add garam masala and cream, allowing it to simmer further for richer flavors. Garnish with chopped cilantro before serving. Enjoy this creamy and flavorful Dal Makhani with naan, rice, or roti! Adjust the spices according to your taste preferences for a delightful experience.
